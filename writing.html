Kerangka Penulisan Lengkap (Task 1 & Task 2)

Panduan struktur paragraf demi paragraf, rumus paraphrase, aturan overview, preposisi data, dan metode PEEL untuk meraih Band 7.0–9.0.
KERANGKA TASK 1Struktur 4 Paragraf Task 1 (Report/Letter)
PARAGRAF 1: INTRODUCTION (Paraphrase Judul Soal)
Target: 1-2 Kalimat (20–30 Kata). Ubah kata-kata soal menggunakan sinonim tanpa mengubah arti.
Rumus Wajib: [Jenis Visual] + [Kata Kerja Paraphrase] + [Subjek/Topik Utama] + [Lokasi/Kategori] + [Tahun & Unit Satuan]
Tabel Paraphrase Otomatis:
Kata di SoalPengganti Band 8-9
The chart shows...The bar chart illustrates / compares / depicts / outlines...
The percentage of...The proportion of / percentage share of...
The number of...The volume of / quantity of / total figure for...
From 2000 to 2010...Over a 10-year period starting in 2000 / between 2000 and 2010...
PARAGRAF 2: OVERVIEW (Kunci Utama Nilai Band 7+)
Target: 2 Kalimat (30–45 Kata). Sebutkan 2 gambaran paling menonjol dari seluruh data.
⚠️ ATURAN EMAS OVERVIEW: DILARANG MEMASUKKAN ANGKA/DATA DETIL DI SINI!
Frasa Pembuka Wajib: "Overall, it is clear that..." atau "At a glance, the most prominent feature is..."
Fokus Per Jenis Visual:
  • Grafik Data (Bar/Line/Pie/Table): Sebutkan mana yang tertinggi/terbesar secara umum & mana yang mengalami kenaikan/penurunan paling dramatis.
  • Process Diagram: Sebutkan total berapa tahap yang ada + dari mana dimulainya dan di mana berakhirnya.
  • Map (Peta): Sebutkan sifat perubahan umum (misal: "area perdesaan berubah total menjadi pemukiman modern").
PARAGRAF 3: BODY 1 (Detail Kelompok Data Utama / Data Tinggi)
Target: 3-4 Kalimat (50–65 Kata). Laporkan data spesifik beserta angka, tahun, dan preposisi yang tepat.
Rumus Preposisi Data:
  • Posisi Awal: "In 2000, food spending stood at / started at 35%..."
  • Perubahan: "...before rising significantly to reach 50% in 2010..." / "...experienced a sharp drop of 15%..."
  • Puncak: "...peaking at an all-time high of 250 grams in 2004..."
PARAGRAF 4: BODY 2 (Detail Kelompok Data Pembanding / Data Rendah)
Target: 3-4 Kalimat (50–65 Kata). Laporkan sisa data atau data yang berlawanan.
Frasa Kontras & Perbandingan:
  • "In sharp contrast, consumption of fish recorded the lowest figure..."
  • "...which was double the proportion of..." / "...accounting for the lion's share of total expenditure."
KERANGKA TASK 2Struktur 4 Paragraf Task 2 (Essay PEEL Method)
PARAGRAF 1: INTRODUCTION (Background + Thesis Statement)
  • Sentence 1 (Background): Paraphrase topik umum dari soal. "It is often argued whether [topic]..."
  • Sentence 2 (Thesis Statement): Nyatakan posisimu secara tegas + 2 alasan utamamu. "In my opinion, I completely agree because it [Reason 1] and [Reason 2]."
PARAGRAF 2 & 3: BODY PARAGRAPHS (Gunakan Rumus P.E.E.L)
  • P - Point (Topic Sentence): Sebutkan ide utamamu di kalimat pertama. ("To begin with, the primary benefit of X is...")
  • E - Explanation: Jelaskan alur sebab-akibat kenapa hal itu bisa terjadi. ("This is because...")
  • E - Example: Berikan contoh nyata / studi kasus. ("For instance, a study by Oxford University showed that...")
  • L - Link: Simpulkan paragraf kembali ke gagasan utama. ("Therefore, it is clear that...")
PARAGRAF 4: CONCLUSION (Rangkuman Akhir)
Target: 2 Kalimat. Rangkum poin-poin utama dan nyatakan kembali thesis statement-mu tanpa memasukkan ide baru.
Frasa Pembuka: "In conclusion, while [drawback], I firmly believe that [main benefit] makes it a positive development."
PART 1 Task 1 Academic & GT Visual Blueprints (8 Types)
1. Bar Chart Master Blueprint (Discrete Categories Comparison)
📊 VISUAL DIAGRAM PREVIEW — BAR CHART
Bar Chart Breakdown & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION (Paraphrase Prompt & Units)
  • Paraphrase the bar chart title. Specify what is being compared across categories and indicate the time period and unit (e.g. percentages, millions of metric tons).
    The bar chart compares the amount of energy produced from five different sources in [Country] in 2010 and 2020...Introductory paraphrase verb
2. OVERVIEW (Highest, Lowest & Key Contrast)
  • Identify the highest bar, lowest bar, or biggest change. *Never include exact numbers here.*
    Overall, petroleum remained the predominant energy source throughout the period, whereas solar power registered the most substantial growth...Highlight dominant category & key growth
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Highest & Major Categories)
  • Group the highest bars or major categories together. Report initial and final values.
    Regarding the dominant energy sectors, petroleum consumption stood at 35 million tons in 2010, before increasing marginally to 38 million in 2020...Report values with prepositions
4. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Lower Categories & Direct Comparisons)
  • Group smaller bars or contrasting categories. Use comparative structures.
    In sharp contrast, solar energy accounted for a negligible 2 million tons in 2010, but surged nearly five-fold to reach 10 million tons by 2020...Comparative contrast phrase
2. Line Graph Master Blueprint (Time-Series Trends & Crossovers)
📈 VISUAL DIAGRAM PREVIEW — LINE GRAPH
Line Graph Breakdown & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION (Paraphrase Trend Subject)
  • State what variables are tracked over time. Specify starting and ending years.
    The line graph depicts changes in the weekly consumption of four types of food in [Nation] over a 25-year period starting in 1979...Time-series intro phrasing
2. OVERVIEW (General Trajectories & Intersections)
  • Highlight overall upward vs downward trajectories and mention key trend crossovers.
    Overall, beef consumption experienced a dramatic decline, whereas chicken consumption surged to become the most popular item by the end of the timeline...General upward/downward summary
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Primary / Falling Trends)
  • Detail the lines showing high or downward movement. Pinpoint starting numbers, dips, and final values.
    In 1979, beef was the highest consumed item at 220 grams per week, but it plummeted steadily to finish at just 100 grams in 2004...Report starting & ending data
4. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Rising / Stable Trends & Crossovers)
  • Detail rising lines and specify the exact year where lines cross each other.
    Conversely, chicken consumption rose steadily, overtaking beef in 1989 and peaking at 250 grams by 2004...Pinpoint line intersection year
3. Pie Chart Master Blueprint (Proportions & Share Distribution)
🥧 VISUAL DIAGRAM PREVIEW — PIE CHART
Pie Chart Breakdown & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION (Proportion Paraphrase)
  • Paraphrase prompt to describe the breakdown of a total (100%).
    The pie charts illustrate the distribution of household expenditure across five key categories in two different years...Proportions & breakdown intro
2. OVERVIEW (Dominant vs Negligible Shares)
  • Highlight the largest sector ("the lion's share") and the smallest sector.
    Overall, housing consistently represented the largest proportion of expenditure, while leisure activities made up the smallest share...Dominant vs minor share summary
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Largest Sectors)
  • Report percentages for major slices using proportion vocabulary (*accounted for, constituted, comprised*).
    Housing accounted for nearly a third of total spending (32%) in 2000, before increasing slightly to 35% in 2015...Proportion verbs + fractions
4. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Smaller Sectors & Shifts)
  • Detail the remaining slices and highlight significant growth or contraction.
    In contrast, food spending experienced a contraction, dropping from 24% to a modest 14% over the same period...Contraction / growth phrasing
4. Table Chart Master Blueprint (Multi-Variable Data Grid)
📋 VISUAL DIAGRAM PREVIEW — DATA TABLE
Table Chart Breakdown & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION (Variables & Categories)
  • Describe what rows and columns represent.
    The table outlines statistical data regarding student performance across three academic subjects in five distinct schools...Data grid introduction
2. OVERVIEW (Maximums, Minimums & Outliers)
  • Identify the highest performing row/column and lowest overall figure.
    Overall, School A achieved the highest average pass rate, whereas Mathematics proved to be the most challenging subject overall...Highest row/column overview
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (High Performing Data Group)
  • Group the highest values together logically. Compare across rows or columns.
    With regard to School A, 88% of students passed English, which was double the pass rate recorded in School E...Cross-table comparative structure
4. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Lower Performing Data Group)
  • Report remaining data points, lowest figures, or outliers.
    At the other end of the scale, School E recorded the lowest results, with Math scores dipping to a minimum of 34%...Lowest figure & minimums
5. Process Diagram / Flowchart Master Blueprint (Step-by-Step Sequence)
🔄 VISUAL DIAGRAM PREVIEW — PROCESS / FLOWCHART
Process / Flowchart Breakdown & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION (Process Summary Paraphrase)
  • State what industrial or natural process is shown.
    The flow diagram illustrates the stage-by-stage process of recycling plastic bottles into synthetic fiber...Process / flowchart intro
2. OVERVIEW (Number of Stages, Start & End Points)
  • Mention the total number of steps, where the process starts, where it ends, and whether it is linear or cyclical.
    Overall, the process comprises eight distinct stages, beginning with the collection of waste plastic and culminating in the production of woven garments...Total stages, start & end points
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Initial Stages 1 to 4)
  • Use Passive Voice and Sequential Connectors (*First, Initially, Subsequently, Once X is done*).
    Initially, plastic bottles are gathered at recycling centers and sorted by material quality. Once sorted, the bottles are crushed into tiny chips...Passive verbs + step sequencers
4. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Final Stages 5 to End)
  • Detail the transformation stages leading to the final product.
    Following this, the melted plastic is extruded through fine nozzles to form threads, which are subsequently woven into fabric rolls...Final transformation connectors
6. Map / Development Diagram Master Blueprint (Geographical Transformations)
🗺️ VISUAL DIAGRAM PREVIEW — MAP / SITE DEVELOPMENT
Map & Site Transformation Breakdown
1. INTRODUCTION (Location & Time Periods)
  • State the location and the years being compared.
    The two maps illustrate the main structural developments that occurred in the coastal village of [Town] between 1995 and 2020...Map comparison intro
2. OVERVIEW (Major Modernization / Loss of Greenery)
  • Highlight the main overall change (e.g. agricultural land turned into residential areas, construction of infrastructure).
    Overall, the village underwent significant modernization, transforming from a largely rural area into a bustling tourist resort...Main transformation overview
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Northern / Eastern Changes)
  • Use directional prepositions (*to the north of, in the eastern section*) and development verbs (*demolished, erected, constructed, expanded*).
    In the northern part of the town, the farmland was completely cleared to make way for a new hotel complex and car park...Directional & development verbs
4. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Southern / Western Changes & Unchanged Elements)
  • Detail changes in the remaining areas and mention any features that stayed original.
    Conversely, the fishing port on the southern coast was demolished, replaced by a modern marina, while the old church remained untouched...Replacement & preserved features
7. Multiple / Mixed Charts Master Blueprint (Combination of 2 Graphics)
📊🥧 VISUAL DIAGRAM PREVIEW — MULTIPLE / MIXED CHARTS
Mixed Charts Breakdown & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION (Paraphrase Both Sources)
  • Write 1-2 sentences introducing Chart 1 and Chart 2 clearly.
    The pie chart details the primary reasons why people move to [City], while the bar chart highlights their level of satisfaction with public services...Dual-source introduction
2. OVERVIEW (1 Key Trend per Graphic)
  • State the main takeaway of Chart 1, then state the main takeaway of Chart 2.
    Overall, employment was the main driver for relocation, whereas healthcare received the highest satisfaction ratings among residents...Dual overview key takeaways
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Full Details of Chart 1)
  • Focus entirely on reporting and grouping the data of Chart 1. Do not mix data yet to keep paragraphs clear.
    Regarding the reasons for moving, employment accounted for the majority of responses at 60%, followed by education at 25%...Chart 1 detailed reporting
4. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Full Details of Chart 2 & Synthesis)
  • Focus entirely on Chart 2 and draw logical connections between Chart 1 and 2 if relevant.
    Turning to public service ratings, 80% of respondents expressed high satisfaction with healthcare, whereas transport scored lower at 40%...Chart 2 detailed reporting
8. GT Formal Complaint Letter Blueprint (General Training Task 1)
GT Formal Letter Breakdown
Salutation: Dear Mr. [Surname] (Dear Sir or Madam if name is unknown)

1. STATEMENT OF PURPOSE (Target: 20-30 words)
  • State clearly and politely why you are writing the letter.
    I am writing to bring to your immediate attention a serious issue regarding...Polite yet firm complaint opening
2. DETAIL THE SITUATION (Target: 60-75 words)
  • Describe the background story, timeline of when it happened, and how it is affecting you.
    The leak began yesterday afternoon, following heavy rainfall...Give specific dates/weather context
    Furthermore, the dampness is causing the plaster to crack, which poses a safety hazard...State subsequent consequences
3. ACTION REQUEST (Target: 40-50 words)
  • State the specific action you expect the recipient to take.
    Therefore, I request that you dispatch a professional technician to repair the roof immediately...State direct action request
Closing & Sign-off:
  • I look forward to your prompt response.
  • Yours sincerely, [Your Name] (or Yours faithfully if saluted Dear Sir/Madam)
PART 2 Task 2 Academic Essay Blueprints (4 Types)
9. Agree / Disagree (Opinion) Essay Blueprint
Opinion Essay Layout & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION PARAGRAPH (Target: 40-50 words)
  • Background Statement: Paraphrase the general topic prompt.
    It is often argued whether [general topic] should be prioritized...Use passive voice to sound formal
  • Thesis Statement: State your clear position and list the two main supporting reasons.
    Personally, I completely agree with this view because it [Reason 1] and [Reason 2]...Your clear stance + roadmap
2. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Primary Argument) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce your first main supporting reason.
    To begin with, the primary advantage of [topic] is that...Transition word + point statement
  • Explanation: Elaborate on how/why this advantage manifests.
    Explain the cause-and-effect flow: "Because of this, society is able to..."
  • Example: Cite a concrete study or real-world example.
    For instance, a recent survey conducted by Oxford University revealed...Pre-framed citation marker
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Secondary Argument) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce your second reason, or address the opposing side.
    Furthermore, another key consideration is that...Adding information transition
  • Explanation & Contrast: Show why alternative paths fail.
    "Without this system, individuals would inevitably struggle to..."
4. CONCLUSION PARAGRAPH (Target: 30-40 words)
  • Restatement of Stance: Summarize both points and reiterate your final opinion.
    In conclusion, while [minor drawback], I believe that the benefits of [Reason 1] make it positive...Strong final re-state
10. Discussion (Discuss Both Views + Opinion) Blueprint
Discussion Essay Layout & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION PARAGRAPH (Target: 40-50 words)
  • Background Statement: Acknowledge that the topic is highly debated.
    Opinions are divided on whether [View A] or if [View B]...Acknowledge division
  • Thesis Statement: State your balanced position.
    In my opinion, while there are valid arguments on both sides, I believe [Your View]...Clear preference marker
2. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (View A - Opposing side) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Topic Sentence: Present the arguments for the side you do not agree with.
    On the one hand, supporters of [View A] argue that...Transition view A
  • Detail & Proof: Explain their rationale.
    "This is because it provides equal opportunities for all members of society..."
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (View B - Your supported side) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Topic Sentence: Present the arguments for your supported view.
    On the other hand, I believe that [View B] offers greater benefits because...Transition view B
  • Explanation: Give details and examples supporting this.
    "For example, Scandinavian countries provide free tuition and have high levels of innovation..."
4. CONCLUSION PARAGRAPH (Target: 30-40 words)
  • Summary & Final Call: Reiterate that both sides are valid but confirm your stance.
    To sum up, although there are reasonable arguments on both sides, the positive impacts of [View B] make it far superior...Summary of views
11. Problem & Solution Essay Blueprint
Problem & Solution Layout & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION PARAGRAPH (Target: 40-50 words)
  • Background & Thesis: Introduce the problem and state that governments/individuals must take immediate action.
    Nowadays, [Problem e.g. traffic congestion] has become a major issue in urban areas. This is primarily caused by [Cause], but it can be tackled by [Solution]...Cause & solution thesis
2. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Causes & Problems) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Detail why the problem exists and its negative consequences.
    The main cause of this issue is the rapid growth of private vehicle ownership...Primary cause statement
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Viable Solutions) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Propose concrete solutions and explain how they fix the cause.
    To address this problem, governments should invest heavily in public transportation networks...Actionable solution proposal
4. CONCLUSION PARAGRAPH (Target: 30-40 words)
  • Summarize the problem-solution link.
    In conclusion, while urban congestion poses severe challenges, targeted investment in public transit offers an effective remedy...Summary of remedies
12. Advantages & Disadvantages Essay Blueprint
Advantages & Disadvantages Layout & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION PARAGRAPH (Target: 40-50 words)
  • Introduce the trend and state whether the advantages outweigh the disadvantages.
    In recent years, remote work has gained widespread popularity. I believe the benefits of flexibility far outweigh the drawbacks of isolation...Outweigh stance statement
2. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Disadvantages / Minor Side) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Acknowledge the drawbacks first.
    On the one hand, a notable drawback of working from home is the blurring of personal and professional boundaries...Drawback statement
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Advantages / Major Side) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Detail the major benefits and show why they carry more weight.
    On the other hand, the primary advantage is the elimination of daily commute stress, saving workers hours every week...Major advantage statement
4. CONCLUSION PARAGRAPH (Target: 30-40 words)
  • Reiterate your final stance.
    In conclusion, despite the risk of isolation, the time saved and enhanced flexibility make remote work a net positive development...Net positive conclusion
Sample 1 (Task 2 Essay): Free University Tuition
PROMPT: Some people think that university education should be free for all students. Discuss both views and give your opinion.
[ Band 9.0 Model Essay ]
It is often argued whether higher education should be fully funded by governments or paid for by the students themselves. In my opinion, while tertiary education is expensive, making it free is crucial for social mobility and economic development.

On the one handCohesive paragraph transition, tuition fees ensure that universities are well-funded. When students pay, institutions have more capital to invest in top-tier research facilities and recruit high-quality professors. For instance, in countries with high tuition like the US, universities dominate global research rankings. However, this fee-paying system inevitably excludes talented low-income youthSophisticated argument contrast, perpetuating social inequality.

On the other handCohesive paragraph contrast, free university education promotes equal opportunity. If access depends solely on academic merit rather than financial wealth, society benefits from a highly skilled workforce. For example, Scandinavian countries offer free tuition and have high levels of innovation. To sum upFormal conclusion signpost, I believe that university education should be free, as the long-term societal and economic benefits far outweigh the funding costs.
Band 9.0 Evaluation & Feedback:
  • Task Response: Flawless task coverage. Position is fully developed and sustained throughout the essay.
  • Coherence & Cohesion: Natural, seamless flow. Linking words are sophisticated and never feel forced.
  • Lexical Resource: Excellent lexical range (*perpetuating, tertiary education, social mobility, merit-based*).
  • Grammar: Highly varied sentence structures showing full flexibility and precision.

Sample 3 (Task 1 GT): Formal Letter - Kitchen Water Leak
PROMPT: Write a letter to your landlord complaining about a water leak in your apartment. Explain the problem, tell him what needs fixing, and request action.

I am writing to bring to your immediate attentionPolite formal complaint opening a serious water leakage issue in the kitchen of my apartment, which is Unit 4B.

The leak began yesterday afternoon, following heavy rainfall. Water is actively dripping from the ceiling directly above the stove area, causing significant pooling on the floor. FurthermoreCohesive marker adding secondary detail, the dampness is causing the plaster on the ceiling to crack and peel, which poses a safety hazard. I have had to turn off the overhead light fixtures in the kitchen to prevent an electrical short-circuit.

ThereforeTransition stating request action, I request that you dispatch a professional plumber to repair the roof or pipes immediately to prevent further structural damage. I also request your approval to have the kitchen ceiling replastered once the leak is sealed.

I look forward to your prompt response and action on this matter.

Yours sincerely,
James Kingston

  • Grammar: Perfect grammatical complexity and execution.
