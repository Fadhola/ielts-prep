Template 1: Agree / Disagree (Opinion) Essay Outline
Introduction:
1. Paraphrase Statement: State the controversial topic in your own words (e.g., 'Some assert that...').
2. Thesis Statement: State your clear position (e.g., 'I completely agree with this proposal because...').

Body Paragraph 1 (Reason 1):
- Point: State the first reason supporting your position.
- Explain: Elaborate on how/why this is the case.
- Example: Give a concrete instance or study proving your point.
- Link: Re-align back to your thesis.

Body Paragraph 2 (Reason 2):
- Point: State the second reason (e.g., 'Furthermore, it is clear that...').
- Explain: Elaborate and provide logical support.
- Example: Give a factual example.
- Link: Re-align.

Conclusion:
- Restatement: Summarize the two main points in one sentence.
- Final Stance: Firmly reiterate your overall opinion.
Template 2: Discussion (Discuss Both Views + Opinion) Outline
Introduction:
1. Paraphrase Statement: Acknowledge that people have differing views on the topic.
2. Thesis Statement: State your own position and briefly outline both sides.

Body Paragraph 1 (View A - Opposing side):
- Point: Explain why some people support the first view.
- Detail: Provide their logical reasons.
- Example: Highlight an example of View A in practice.

Body Paragraph 2 (View B - Your supported side):
- Point: Explain the arguments for the second view.
- Detail: Explain why this view is stronger.
- Example: Provide an example showing the benefits.

Conclusion:
- Summary: Re-state that both sides have valid points, but firmly conclude in favor of your opinion.
Template 3: Task 1 Academic Line Graph / Chart Report
Paragraph 1: Introduction
- Sentence: Paraphrase the graph prompt (e.g., 'The line graph depicts the volume of... between [Year] and [Year] in [Location].'). Include units.

Paragraph 2: Overview
- Sentence 1: Identify the main overall trend (e.g., 'Overall, the sales of [Product A] experienced an upward trend, whereas [Product B] dropped.').
- Sentence 2: Highlight the peak or the most dramatic change. *Do not write specific numbers here.*

Paragraph 3: Body Paragraph 1 (Category A Detail)
- Report starting numbers, progress, and end points for the first category. Group similar trends together.

Paragraph 4: Body Paragraph 2 (Category B Detail)
- Contrast the first category with the second category. Mention exact numbers and years in brackets or using 'stood at' or 'rose to'.
Template 4: Task 1 GT Formal Complaint Letter Layout
Salutation: Dear Sir or Madam, (or Dear Mr./Ms. [Surname] if known)

Paragraph 1: Purpose of the letter
- State clearly why you are writing (e.g., 'I am writing to bring to your attention a serious issue regarding...').

Paragraph 2: Detailed explanation of the problem (Bullet 1 & 2)
- Outline the exact problem, when it occurred, and how it is affecting you (e.g., 'The central heating system has failed, resulting in freezing...').

Paragraph 3: Proposed solution and action request (Bullet 3)
- State what action you expect (e.g., 'Therefore, I request that a technician be dispatched immediately...').

Sign-off & Closing:
- I look forward to your prompt response.
- Yours faithfully, (use if starting Dear Sir/Madam) OR Yours sincerely, (use if you know their name)
- [Your Name]
Sample 1 (Task 2): Discussion Essay - Free University Tuition
PROMPT: Some people think that university education should be free for all students. Discuss both views and give your opinion.
It is often argued whether higher education should be fully funded by governments or paid for by the students themselves. In my opinion, while tertiary education is expensive, making it free is crucial for social mobility and economic development.

On the one handParagraph transition view A, tuition fees ensure that universities are well-funded. When students pay, institutions have more capital to invest in top-tier research facilities and recruit high-quality professors. For instance, in countries with high tuition like the US, universities dominate global research rankings. HoweverConcession contrast connector, this system excludes talented low-income youth.

On the other handParagraph transition view B, free university education promotes equal opportunity. If access depends solely on academic merit rather than financial wealth, society benefits from a highly skilled workforce. For example, Scandinavian countries offer free tuition and have high levels of innovation. To sum upConclusion signpost, I believe that university education should be free, as the long-term societal and economic benefits far outweigh the funding costs.
Band 9.0 Evaluation & Analysis:
  • Task Response: Completely addresses both sides of the argument and gives a clear opinion. Ideas are fully developed with examples.
  • Coherence & Cohesion: Paragraphs flow logically. Uses transition markers (*On the one hand, Conversely, For instance, To sum up*) effectively.
  • Lexical Resource: High-quality vocabulary used accurately (*tertiary education, social mobility, academic merit, innovate*).
  • Grammar: Error-free range of structures including conditionals and relative clauses.
Sample 2 (Task 1 Academic): Bar Chart Report - Pupil Expenditures
PROMPT: The bar chart compares the average annual expenditure per pupil in three different school levels in the UK between 2000 and 2015.
The bar chart illustrates the average annual spending per student across primary, secondary, and tertiary levels of education in the United Kingdom over a fifteen-year period from 2000 to 2015.

OverallOverview signpost, it is clear that secondary schools received the highest funding per pupil throughout the entire period, while primary schools consistently received the lowest. Additionally, expenditures for primary and secondary education experienced overall growth, whereas spending on tertiary education declined.

Regarding primary education, average spending stood atPreposition for static figure £3,000 per pupil in 2000, before rising steadily to reachDescribes progressive upward change £4,500 in 2015. Similarly, funding for secondary school pupils climbed from £5,000 in 2000 to peak at £6,800 in 2010, before leveling off at £6,500 at the end of the period.

In contrastContrast transition link, tertiary education saw a different trend. Spending initially stood at £6,000 per student in 2000, which was higher than primary levels. HoweverContrast sentence connector, it subsequently fell to £4,800 in 2010, before registering a minor recovery to end at £5,200 in 2015.
Band 9.0 Evaluation & Analysis:
  • Task Achievement: Accurate overview statement summarizing major trends. Key comparisons are made with precise numbers and years.
  • Cohesion: Paragraph structures are logical. Transitions like *Overall*, *Similarly*, *In contrast*, and *Subsequently* guide the reader.
  • Vocabulary: High precision (*per student, expenditure, leveled off, minor recovery*).
