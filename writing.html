PART 1 Task 1 Report & Letter Blueprints
Template 3: Task 1 Academic Line Graph Blueprint
Academic Report Layout & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION PARAGRAPH (Target: 20-30 words)
  • Introduction Sentence: Paraphrase the prompt using synonyms. Include geographical data and years.
    The line graph depicts the weekly consumption of [Product] in [Location] between [Year] and [Year]...Use active verbs: depicts, illustrates
2. OVERVIEW PARAGRAPH (Target: 30-40 words)
  • Core Trend Statement: Identify the main overall trends. Point out rises, drops, or stable patterns. *Do not write numbers here.*
    Overall, the consumption of [A] experienced a dramatic decline, whereas [B] climbed...Highlight contrasting trends
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Detailed Trends) (Target: 70-80 words)
  • Report starting points and progression for the primary categories. Group similar trends together.
    Regarding primary items, average consumption stood at [X grams] in 1979 before falling to [Y grams] in 2004...Report values using prepositions
4. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Contrasting Trends) (Target: 70-80 words)
  • Report starting points and final values for the remaining categories. Add comparisons.
    In contrast, the consumption of [C] climbed steadily, overtaking [A] in [Year] to peak at [Z grams]...Compare crossovers/peaks
Template 4: Task 1 GT Formal Complaint Letter Blueprint
GT Letter Layout & Phrasing
Salutation: Dear Mr. [Surname] (Dear Sir or Madam if name is unknown)

1. STATEMENT OF PURPOSE (Target: 20-30 words)
  • State clearly and politely why you are writing the letter.
    I am writing to bring to your immediate attention a serious issue regarding...Polite yet firm complaint opening
2. DETAIL THE SITUATION (Target: 60-75 words)
  • Describe the background story, timeline of when it happened, and how it is affecting you.
    The leak began yesterday afternoon, following heavy rainfall...Give specific dates/weather context
    Furthermore, the dampness is causing the plaster to crack, which poses a safety hazard...State subsequent consequences
3. ACTION REQUEST (Target: 40-50 words)
  • State the specific action you expect the recipient to take.
    Therefore, I request that you dispatch a professional technician to repair the roof immediately...State direct action request
Closing & Sign-off:
  • I look forward to your prompt response.
  • Yours sincerely, [Your Name] (or Yours faithfully if saluted Dear Sir/Madam)
PART 2 Task 2 Academic Essay Blueprints
Template 1: Agree / Disagree (Opinion) Essay Blueprint
Opinion Essay Layout & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION PARAGRAPH (Target: 40-50 words)
  • Background Statement: Paraphrase the general topic prompt.
    It is often argued whether [general topic] should be prioritized...Use passive voice to sound formal
  • Thesis Statement: State your clear position and list the two main supporting reasons.
    Personally, I completely agree with this view because it [Reason 1] and [Reason 2]...Your clear stance + roadmap
2. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (Primary Argument) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce your first main supporting reason.
    To begin with, the primary advantage of [topic] is that...Transition word + point statement
  • Explanation: Elaborate on how/why this advantage manifests.
    Explain the cause-and-effect flow: "Because of this, society is able to..."
  • Example: Cite a concrete study or real-world example.
    For instance, a recent survey conducted by Oxford University revealed...Pre-framed citation marker
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (Secondary Argument) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Topic Sentence: Introduce your second reason, or address the opposing side.
    Furthermore, another key consideration is that...Adding information transition
  • Explanation & Contrast: Show why alternative paths fail.
    "Without this system, individuals would inevitably struggle to..."
4. CONCLUSION PARAGRAPH (Target: 30-40 words)
  • Restatement of Stance: Summarize both points and reiterate your final opinion.
    In conclusion, while [minor drawback], I believe that the benefits of [Reason 1] make it positive...Strong final re-state
Template 2: Discussion (Discuss Both Views + Opinion) Blueprint
Discussion Essay Layout & Phrasing
1. INTRODUCTION PARAGRAPH (Target: 40-50 words)
  • Background Statement: Acknowledge that the topic is highly debated.
    Opinions are divided on whether [View A] or if [View B]...Acknowledge division
  • Thesis Statement: State your balanced position.
    In my opinion, while there are valid arguments on both sides, I believe [Your View]...Clear preference marker
2. BODY PARAGRAPH 1 (View A - Opposing side) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Topic Sentence: Present the arguments for the side you do not agree with.
    On the one hand, supporters of [View A] argue that...Transition view A
  • Detail & Proof: Explain their rationale.
    "This is because it provides equal opportunities for all members of society..."
3. BODY PARAGRAPH 2 (View B - Your supported side) (Target: 90-100 words)
  • Topic Sentence: Present the arguments for your supported view.
    On the other hand, I believe that [View B] offers greater benefits because...Transition view B
  • Explanation: Give details and examples supporting this.
    "For example, Scandinavian countries provide free tuition and have high levels of innovation..."
4. CONCLUSION PARAGRAPH (Target: 30-40 words)
  • Summary & Final Call: Reiterate that both sides are valid but confirm your stance.
    To sum up, although there are reasonable arguments on both sides, the positive impacts of [View B] make it far superior...Summary of views
Sample 1 (Task 2 Essay): Free University Tuition
PROMPT: Some people think that university education should be free for all students. Discuss both views and give your opinion.
[ Band 9.0 Model Essay ]
It is often argued whether higher education should be fully funded by governments or paid for by the students themselves. In my opinion, while tertiary education is expensive, making it free is crucial for social mobility and economic development.

On the one handCohesive paragraph transition, tuition fees ensure that universities are well-funded. When students pay, institutions have more capital to invest in top-tier research facilities and recruit high-quality professors. For instance, in countries with high tuition like the US, universities dominate global research rankings. However, this fee-paying system inevitably excludes talented low-income youthSophisticated argument contrast, perpetuating social inequality.

On the other handCohesive paragraph contrast, free university education promotes equal opportunity. If access depends solely on academic merit rather than financial wealth, society benefits from a highly skilled workforce. For example, Scandinavian countries offer free tuition and have high levels of innovation. To sum upFormal conclusion signpost, I believe that university education should be free, as the long-term societal and economic benefits far outweigh the funding costs.
Band 9.0 Evaluation & Feedback:
  • Task Response: Flawless task coverage. Position is fully developed and sustained throughout the essay.
  • Coherence & Cohesion: Natural, seamless flow. Linking words are sophisticated and never feel forced.
  • Lexical Resource: Excellent lexical range (*perpetuating, tertiary education, social mobility, merit-based*).
  • Grammar: Highly varied sentence structures showing full flexibility and precision.
Sample 2 (Task 1 Academic): Line Graph - Meat & Fish Consumption
PROMPT: The line graph shows the weekly consumption of fish and different kinds of meat in a European country between 1979 and 2004.
[ Band 9.0 Model Essay ]
The line graph depicts the consumption of three types of meat and fish in a European nation over a 25-year period starting in 1979.

Overall, beef consumption experienced a dramatic decline, whereas chicken consumption surged to become the most popular protein sourcePrecise descriptions of trend crossover by the end of the period. Meanwhile, pork and fish consumption remained relatively stable.

In 1979, beef was the most consumed item, standing at 220 grams per weekAdvanced prepositional structure. However, it dropped sharply over the years to finish at just 100 grams in 2004. Conversely, chicken consumption steadily climbed, overtaking beef in 1989Accurate pinpointing of chart intersection and peaking at 250 grams by the end of the timeline.

Regarding pork and fish, their numbers remained comparatively low. Pork consumption fluctuated marginally around 75 to 90 grams, while fish saw a slight decrease from 55 grams to end the period at 48 grams.
Band 9.0 Evaluation & Feedback:
  • Task Achievement: Outstanding summary of data trends. Selects key crossover years (1989) and makes flawless comparisons.
  • Coherence & Cohesion: Information is organized perfectly into logical comparisons. Transitions are varied (*Conversely, Meanwhile, Regarding*).
  • Lexical Resource: Precise vocabulary (*depicts, surged, marginally, standing at*).
  • Grammar: Flawless grammatical range, using participle clauses and complex comparisons.
Sample 3 (Task 1 GT): Formal Letter - Kitchen Water Leak
PROMPT: Write a letter to your landlord complaining about a water leak in your apartment. Explain the problem, tell him what needs fixing, and request action.
[ Band 9.0 Model Letter ]
Dear Mr. Davies,

I am writing to bring to your immediate attentionPolite formal complaint opening a serious water leakage issue in the kitchen of my apartment, which is Unit 4B.

The leak began yesterday afternoon, following heavy rainfall. Water is actively dripping from the ceiling directly above the stove area, causing significant pooling on the floor. FurthermoreCohesive marker adding secondary detail, the dampness is causing the plaster on the ceiling to crack and peel, which poses a safety hazard. I have had to turn off the overhead light fixtures in the kitchen to prevent an electrical short-circuit.

ThereforeTransition stating request action, I request that you dispatch a professional plumber to repair the roof or pipes immediately to prevent further structural damage. I also request your approval to have the kitchen ceiling replastered once the leak is sealed.

I look forward to your prompt response and action on this matter.

Yours sincerely,
James Kingston
Band 9.0 Evaluation & Feedback:
  • Task Achievement: Fully answers all points with perfect, highly persuasive formal tone.
  • Coherence: Seamless linking of ideas across paragraphs.
  • Lexical Resource: Sophisticated, precise vocabulary (*bring to your attention, safety hazard, dispatch, structural damage*).
  • Grammar: Perfect grammatical complexity and execution.
