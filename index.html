PART 1Short Familiar Questions
Point → Extend → Example (3–4 sentences max)
  1. Point: answer the question directly, no dodging.
  2. Extend: add one reason, feeling, or detail (why / how / when).
  3. Example (optional): one short concrete instance if it fits naturally.
Example — "Do you enjoy your work?" "No, not really. I struggle with the technical math side, so I lean on AI tools a lot to get through my tasks."

Longer isn't better here — 20–30 seconds is the target. Over-explaining reads as memorized.

PART 2Cue Card (Long Turn)
Intro → Bullet-by-bullet → Closing (fill the full ~2 minutes)
  1. Intro (1 sentence): name the person/place/thing right away.
  2. Work through every bullet point in order — don't skip any.
  3. For each bullet, add one personal detail or feeling so it doesn't sound like a list.
  4. Closing (1 sentence): tie it back to why it still matters now.
Example — closing line "That's why this project still matters to me today — it's the proof I can build something real on my own."

Running out of things to say before time's up? Go back and elaborate one bullet further — never go silent.

PART 3Discussion Questions
Opinion → Reasoning → Example / Counterpoint
  1. Opinion: take a clear stance immediately — don't hedge from the start.
  2. Reasoning: explain why ("because...").
  3. Example or nuance: a real-world instance, or acknowledge the other side.
Example — "Will AI replace jobs?" "I think AI will replace some jobs, but not eliminate them entirely. Repetitive tasks are most at risk, but roles that need judgment — like AI engineering itself, ironically — still need humans to interpret the output."

Showing you can see two sides scores higher than a flat yes/no. Nuance signals band 7+ thinking.

PART 1Sample Answers by Band
Question: "Do you enjoy your work?"
BAND 6basic, some errors
▶
Yes, I enjoy my work, but sometimes it is difficult for me. I work as AI engineer1 and I use many AI tools for finish2 my task. I like it because I can learn new things everyday3.
  1. 1errorMissing article — should be "as an AI engineer."
  2. 2errorWrong verb form after preposition — should be "to finish."
  3. 3error"Everyday" is an adjective; the adverbial form needed here is two words — "every day."
  4. ·general"Enjoy," "like," and "difficult" repeat with no synonym variation — this caps the vocabulary score even though meaning is clear.
BAND 7flexible, minor slips
▶
On the whole1, yes — I do enjoy it, although the technical side can be quite challenging2 for me. I work as an AI engineer, and I rely heavily on3 AI tools to get through some of the more difficult tasks. What I really like is that4 I'm constantly learning something new.
  1. 1cohesionOpens with a natural discourse marker instead of a flat "yes" — signals more control than Band 6.
  2. 2cohesionAn "although" clause handled cleanly shows comfort with complex sentence structure.
  3. 3vocab"Rely heavily on" is more precise and natural than "use a lot."
  4. 4cohesionA cleft sentence ("What I really like is...") adds variety instead of just starting with "I like."
BAND 8natural, idiomatic
▶
Genuinely, yes, though it's a mixed bag1. The maths-heavy side of AI engineering isn't really my strength2, so I've come to depend quite a lot on AI tools themselves to bridge that gap3 — which is a bit ironic, if you think about it4. But overall, the constant learning curve is what keeps me engaged.
  1. 1idiom"A mixed bag" — natural idiom used accurately, not just inserted for effect.
  2. 2vocabA tactful, softer way to admit a weakness than "I'm bad at" — shows register awareness.
  3. 3idiom"Bridge that gap" — figurative language deployed with precision.
  4. 4cohesionThis self-aware aside signals real-time reflective thinking rather than a rehearsed script — a key Band 8 marker.
BAND 9native-like precision
▶
Honestly?1 It's a mixed bag. I wouldn't say the technical rigour2 comes naturally to me — the maths side, especially — but I've leaned into3 using AI tools to close that gap, which, admittedly,4 is a little ironic given the field I'm in. That said, the constant intellectual stretch5 is exactly what keeps me hooked6.
  1. 1cohesionA one-word rhetorical opener — a natural spoken-English habit that's very hard to fake or memorize.
  2. 2vocab"Technical rigour" — precise, register-appropriate academic vocabulary.
  3. 3idiom"Leaned into" — natural phrasal verb, used with full control.
  4. 4cohesionThe embedded qualifier "which, admittedly," shows total ease with complex clause structures.
  5. 5vocab"Intellectual stretch" — vivid, precise phrasing over a generic word like "challenge."
  6. 6idiom"Keeps me hooked" — natural idiomatic closing that ties the tone together.
