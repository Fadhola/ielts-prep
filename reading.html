Passage 1: Avian Intelligence & Adaptability
Observations of different species of birds, both in the wild and in captivity, have shown a great deal of evidence of high levels of avian intelligence.Thesis statement of passage For years, scientists believed that only primates were capable of complex tool construction and problem solving. However, recent experiments with New Caledonian crows have shattered this biological assumption. These birds have been observed bending straight pieces of wire to manufacture hooksClue for Q1 (crows behavior), which they subsequently use to retrieve food from narrow chambers.

In addition to tool-making, cognitive mapping has been observed in species of migratory birds. The birds navigate thousands of miles with remarkable precision by mapping star clusters and the Earth's magnetic fieldsClue for Q2 (navigation), adjusting their trajectory based on real-time weather anomalies.
Questions & Answers
Q: What physical actions performed by New Caledonian crows demonstrate cognitive problem-solving?
Answer Explanation
Manufacturing hooks by bending straight wire to retrieve food from chambers.
Q: What natural elements do migratory birds map to navigate accurately?
Answer Explanation
Star clusters and the Earth's magnetic fields.
Passage 2: The Pathophysiology of OCD
Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is a clinically diagnosed anxiety disorder characterized by distressing and intrusive thoughts, known as obsessions. To combat these excessive impulses, most OCD sufferers perform repetitive, time-consuming rituals (compulsions) that they believe will temporarily relieve their intense anxiety.Clue for Q1 (compulsion purpose) These rituals can include compulsive washing, meticulous checking, or repetitive counting.

Neurological research reveals that OCD is linked to communication malfunctions between the frontal lobe of the brain and the deeper basal ganglia.Clue for Q2 (brain regions) Specifically, the brain's filtering mechanism fails to dismiss non-essential warning signals, causing the patient to experience an ongoing state of emergency.
Questions & Answers
Q: According to the text, what is the primary purpose of compulsive rituals?
Answer Explanation
To temporarily relieve intense anxiety caused by excessive thoughts or impulses.
Q: What specific brain areas have communication failures in OCD patients?
Answer Explanation
The connection between the frontal lobe and the basal ganglia.
Passage 3: Zulu Beadwork as a Form of Communication
In the Zulu nation of Southern Africa, beadwork has long served as a primary medium for social communication and cultural expression. Historically, Zulu girls hand-crafted beadwork items for themselves, their peer groups, and the young men they admired. The elaborate colors and geometric layouts of the beads communicate specific messages regarding a person's marital status, intentions, and emotional feelings.

Geometric shapes act as a structured code. For example, a single triangle with the apex pointing downward denotes an unmarried man.Clue for Q1 (unmarried man symbol) Conversely, two triangles connected apex-to-apex in an hourglass shape symbolize a married man, while married women wear beadwork featuring triangles forming a diamond shape.Clue for Q2 (married woman symbol)
Questions & Answers
Q: What geometric symbol indicates that a Zulu man is unmarried?
Answer Explanation
A single triangle with the apex pointing downward.
Q: Who historically crafted the beadwork items in Zulu society?
Answer Explanation
Zulu girls.
